The Iron Giant, 1999, Warner Bros. Feature Animation

The Iron Giant is a 1999 American animated science fiction comedy-drama film using both traditional animation and computer animation, produced by Warner Bros. Feature Animation and directed by Brad Bird in his directorial debut. It is based on the 1968 novel The Iron Man by Ted Hughes (which was published in the United States as The Iron Giant).

Directed by Brad Bird

The Iron Giant was Brad Bird's directorial debut. Brad went on to direct a number of critically-acclaimed films including The Incredibles, Ratatouille and Mission Impossible: Ghost Protocol.

Based upon "The Iron Man"

The Iron Man: A Children's Story in Five Nights is a 1968 science fiction novel by British Poet Laureate Ted Hughes. The first North American edition, also published in 1968, had its main title was changed to The Iron Giant.

Starring...

The movie stars the voice acting talents of Eli Marienthal, Christopher McDonald, Jennifer Aniston, Harry Connick, Jr., John Mahoney, Vin Diesel, James Gammon, M. Emmet Walsh and Cloris Leachman.

Brief Plot Summary

How'd It Do?

A Failure in Theaters but a Critical Darling

The theatrical release only garnered a total of $31,333,917 worldwide despite widespread critical acclaim from both critics and audiences. This largely due to a lack of marketing. However, it has since garnered a cult following. (source)

 

The Iron Giant is one of my favorite animated movies of all time. It combines a fantastic story with beautifully hand-drawn animation.

One of my favorite things about the movie is its distinctive art style. It combined aspects of classic animation, including Disney, with 1950s illustration - drawing further inspiration from the art of Norman Rockwell, Edward Hopper and N.C. Wyeth.

But the movie isn't just beautiful to watch. It is a fantastic story full of complex themes that is wonderfully well acted. I'm not afraid to admit that the climactic scene always leaves me a little teary-eyed - and I've seen this movie countless times.
